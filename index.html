Introduction

Life is a flow of time. In the Bhagavad Gita the Lord says, "I am Time." When people waste their time unnecessarily, they forget that they are wasting their life. Time wasted is life wasted. This life is a gift of God, and we should utilize this life intelligently for self-improvement and inner transformation.

Through positive thoughts, inspiring talk and words, and productive work, one can use time fruitfully and live a loving lifestyle of beauty and perfection.

Many people write daily in a diary, keeping note of their activities. Many spiritual masters have advised seekers to write a spiritual diary to keep a strict note of their daily activities and practice, and to use it for their self-analysis. It is a simple way of self-analysis and assessment for self-advancement.

This Kriya self-analysis diary is a simple chart with relevant questions to be answered daily, before going to bed, with a yes or no, by assigning a number or percentage, or rating the question on a scale of 1-10. There are also other questions for contemplation. This booklet offers some background to the questions and the perspective from which to consider these questions.

Some Things to Remember

  1. Consider and complete the questions with a prayerful attitude.
  2. You are your best friend. Be sincere while completing it.
  3. Retire with prayer and forgiveness.
  4. You can assess your actions weekly or monthly.
  5. Do not develop a demeaning attitude toward yourself because of failure to attain a goal. Failures are the pillars of success.
  6. Begin the new month with a more positive approach and determination.
  7. Remember the teaching of Gurudev, Practice, practice, and practice. One day you will behold the divine goal.
  8. No effort goes in vain. Today or tomorrow it will produce fruit. Pray to God and the masters for your self-development.
  9. Remember always that we are children of God, in spite of mistakes and weaknesses.
